## Why use an ACT mortgage broker

The ACT property market has characteristics that set it apart from the rest of Australia. Canberra consistently ranks among the highest median household income cities in the country, which influences borrowing capacity, property values, and the types of loans that work best for local buyers.

With Auri Finance, you gain access to a panel of more than 40 lenders. This means we can identify products suited to the ACT market, whether you need a loan for a high value established home in Griffith, a new build in one of Canberra's growth suburbs, or an investment property near the Australian National University.

Not all lenders assess ACT properties in the same way. Some have specific policies around unit complexes, crown leases (which apply to most Canberra land), and government employee income. We understand these details and match you with lenders whose policies align with your circumstances, reducing delays and improving your chances of approval.

## ACT property market

Canberra's property market is underpinned by a stable and well paid workforce, largely driven by the Australian Public Service and associated industries. This creates consistent demand for housing and relatively low unemployment, which in turn supports property values.

The ACT has seen steady growth across established suburbs such as Woden, Belconnen, and Tuggeranong, while newer developments in Molonglo Valley, Whitlam, and Taylor have attracted first home buyers with modern housing and competitive pricing.

Canberra's rental market is tight, with vacancy rates historically sitting well below the national average. This makes the ACT attractive for property investors seeking reliable tenants and strong rental yields. Suburbs close to government hubs, universities, and hospitals tend to perform particularly well.

The ACT government has implemented stamp duty reforms, progressively reducing stamp duty for buyers while transitioning to a land tax model. First home buyers in the ACT can also access the Home Buyer Concession Scheme, which provides significant stamp duty savings for eligible purchasers.
