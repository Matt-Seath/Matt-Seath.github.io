## Property Landscape in Sunnybank Hills

The housing stock in Sunnybank Hills is predominantly freestanding homes, many of which sit on generous blocks of 600 square metres or more. You will find a mix of older brick and tile homes built in the 1970s and 1980s alongside renovated properties and newer builds. Townhouse developments have also emerged in recent years, providing more affordable entry points for buyers. The streets are generally wide and tree lined, contributing to the suburb's reputation as a pleasant and family focused place to live. Property prices tend to offer solid value compared to suburbs closer to the city centre, which keeps demand strong across buyer categories.

## Who Buys in Sunnybank Hills?

Families are the dominant buyer group here, attracted by the combination of larger homes, quality local schools, and safe, quiet streets. Runcorn State High School and several well regarded primary schools sit within easy reach. First home buyers also find opportunities in the suburb, particularly among townhouses and older homes that offer scope for improvement. Investors appreciate the suburb's consistently low vacancy rates and reliable rental returns, fuelled by demand from families and working professionals. Retirees looking to stay in a familiar community while downsizing from larger homes also contribute to local turnover.
