## Buying property in Sunnybank

Sunnybank sits approximately 13 kilometres south of the Brisbane CBD in the 4109 postcode, within the Brisbane City Council boundary. The suburb is well known for its multicultural community, with Market Square and Sunnybank Plaza serving as major local hubs for shopping, dining, and services.

**Property types:** The suburb offers a mix of established brick and timber homes on 600sqm+ blocks, modern townhouse complexes, and low rise apartments. Older homes on larger lots continue to attract buyers interested in renovation or future development potential. Newer townhouses and units provide a more affordable entry point.

**Typical price ranges:** Detached houses in Sunnybank generally range from $800,000 to $1,200,000 depending on block size, condition, and proximity to key amenities. Townhouses typically sit between $500,000 and $700,000. Units start from around $350,000, making them accessible for first home buyers and investors.

**Rental market:** Sunnybank has a strong rental market driven by demand from students attending nearby Griffith University (Nathan campus, approximately 3km away), healthcare workers at the QE II Hospital precinct, and families who prefer to rent in the area for its schools and lifestyle. Rental yields for units and townhouses in Sunnybank tend to sit between 4.5% and 5.5%.

## Who buys in Sunnybank

**First home buyers:** Units and townhouses under $600,000 are within reach for many first home buyers, particularly those eligible for the Queensland First Home Owner Grant ($30,000 for new builds) and stamp duty concessions. A couple with a combined income of $150,000 and a $60,000 deposit could borrow approximately $650,000, putting a large portion of Sunnybank's townhouse market within reach.

**Families:** Established houses near Sunnybank State High School, Sunnybank State School, and Runcorn State High School attract families looking for good school catchments without the price premium of inner city suburbs.

**Investors:** Proximity to Griffith University, the Sunnybank commercial precinct, and strong bus connections to the CBD make Sunnybank a reliable investment area. We help investors structure loans with offset accounts and interest only periods where appropriate.

**Downsizers:** Residents in surrounding suburbs like Stretton and Calamvale sometimes downsize into Sunnybank townhouses or units to be closer to shops, transport, and medical services.

## Transport and infrastructure

Sunnybank does not have its own train station, but is well served by bus routes along Mains Road and McCullough Street connecting to the Brisbane CBD, Garden City, and surrounding suburbs. The nearest train stations are Altandi (2.5km) and Sunnybank station (technically in the neighbouring area, 2km).

The Pacific Motorway (M3) is easily accessible, providing quick connections south to Logan and the Gold Coast, and north to the CBD and Brisbane Airport.

## What to consider when buying in Sunnybank

**Flooding:** Some parts of Sunnybank, particularly lower lying areas near Bulimba Creek, have flood mapping overlays. This can affect insurance costs and lender willingness. We know which lenders are comfortable with flood affected postcodes and which ones apply restrictions.

**Character vs new build:** If you are buying a character home built before 1946, you may be eligible for a character overlay that affects what future development is possible on the block. This matters if you are buying with the intention to subdivide or develop later.

**High density zoning:** Parts of Sunnybank near the commercial precinct are zoned for medium to high density development. This can be an opportunity for investors but also means your neighbourhood may change over time. Check the Brisbane City Plan zoning map before making an offer.

## Frequently asked questions

**How much deposit do I need to buy in Sunnybank?**
The minimum is typically 5% of the purchase price. For a $700,000 townhouse, that is $35,000. With less than 20% ($140,000), you will usually pay Lenders Mortgage Insurance unless you qualify for a government guarantee scheme. The First Home Guarantee lets eligible buyers avoid LMI with just 5%.

**What are the stamp duty costs for Sunnybank?**
For a $700,000 established home in Queensland, stamp duty is approximately $17,350. First home buyers may be eligible for a concession that reduces this significantly. For a new home under $550,000, the concession can eliminate stamp duty entirely.
