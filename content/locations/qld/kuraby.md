## Why buy in Kuraby

Kuraby strikes an appealing balance between affordability and location. It is close enough to the Brisbane CBD for a reasonable commute, with Kuraby train station providing a direct rail link into the city. The suburb is also well positioned for drivers, with easy access to the Pacific Motorway and surrounding employment hubs.

Local amenities include parks, sporting fields, and bushland reserves that give the suburb a green, open feel. The nearby Karawatha Forest provides a natural escape within minutes of home. Schools in and around Kuraby are popular with families, and shopping is convenient with several centres a short drive away, including Sunnybank Hills Shoppingtown and Westfield Garden City.

Property values in Kuraby have shown steady growth over recent years, supported by consistent demand from both owner occupiers and investors. The suburb's affordability relative to neighbouring areas makes it an attractive entry point for buyers looking for value in the southern corridor.

## Property types in Kuraby

Kuraby features a predominantly residential landscape of detached houses on standard suburban blocks. Many homes were built in the 1980s and 1990s, offering solid construction with scope for renovation or modernisation. You will find a range of three and four bedroom homes, often with generous backyards and single or double garages.

In recent years, some older properties have been replaced by newer builds or subdivided to create townhouse developments. This adds variety to the market and provides options for buyers at different price points, from established character homes through to contemporary townhouses.

## Who buys in Kuraby

First home buyers are a significant presence in Kuraby, attracted by the relatively affordable entry prices compared to suburbs closer to the CBD. The train station is a major drawcard for commuters who prefer public transport over driving.

Families make up a large portion of the resident population, valuing the quiet streets, green spaces, and nearby school options. Investors also see opportunity in Kuraby, as rental demand is strong and the suburb's fundamentals, including transport, amenities, and ongoing development in South Brisbane, support long term capital growth.
