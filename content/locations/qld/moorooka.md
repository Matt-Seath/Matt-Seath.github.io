## Why buy in Moorooka

Moorooka delivers inner city convenience without the inner city price tag. The suburb has its own train station on the Beenleigh line, making commuting to the CBD straightforward. Bus routes along Beaudesert Road add further public transport options, and drivers benefit from quick access to the Pacific Motorway and surrounding arterial roads.

The suburb's character is one of its greatest assets. Moorooka retains a village feel with local shops, cafes, and community spaces that foster a sense of belonging. Nearby Toohey Forest provides a large natural bushland reserve perfect for walking and cycling. For everyday needs, residents can easily reach the shopping and dining precincts at Sunnybank and Annerley.

Moorooka has attracted increasing attention from buyers who recognise the value on offer in a suburb so close to the city. Capital growth has been strong as more people discover what locals have known for years: Moorooka is a great place to live.

## Property types in Moorooka

Moorooka is rich with character homes, including Queenslanders and post war cottages that sit on elevated blocks with pleasant outlooks. These properties are highly sought after by buyers who appreciate traditional architecture and the opportunity to renovate and add value.

Alongside the older homes, you will find a selection of modern townhouses and small apartment buildings that cater to different buyer needs. The mix of housing options means Moorooka suits everyone from single professionals wanting a compact unit to families seeking a classic Brisbane home with a big backyard.

## Who buys in Moorooka

Moorooka draws a diverse range of buyers. Young couples and first home buyers see it as an affordable way to get into the inner south market, particularly those who have been priced out of nearby suburbs like Tarragindi or Annerley. The character homes attract renovators and buyers who want a home with personality and history.

Families appreciate the quiet residential streets, access to schools, and the spacious lots that many older properties offer. Investors find Moorooka appealing for its proximity to the CBD, strong tenant demand, and the potential for capital growth as the suburb continues to rise in popularity.
