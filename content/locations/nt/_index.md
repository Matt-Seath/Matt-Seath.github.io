## Why use an NT mortgage broker

The Northern Territory presents specific lending challenges that not every broker or bank handles well. Some lenders restrict lending in certain NT postcodes, apply different valuations for properties in tropical climates, or have reservations about smaller regional markets. At Auri Finance, we know which lenders are comfortable lending in the Territory and which ones to approach for the best terms.

Our panel of over 40 lenders gives you a significant advantage over approaching a single bank. We can identify products that suit the NT market, including loans for properties built to cyclone standards, elevated homes common in the Top End, and larger rural residential blocks that some lenders consider non standard.

Many NT residents work in government, defence, mining, or health sectors, often with allowances and variable income components. We understand how different lenders assess these income types and can match you with a lender whose policies work in your favour.

## NT property market

Darwin is the most affordable capital city in Australia by median house price, which presents a compelling opportunity for first home buyers and investors. The market experienced a prolonged downturn following the end of the Inpex construction boom but has shown signs of recovery as population growth returns and rental markets tighten.

Popular areas for buyers in Darwin include the northern suburbs of Nightcliff, Rapid Creek, and Millner, as well as the satellite city of Palmerston. The Darwin waterfront precinct and inner suburbs appeal to professionals seeking modern apartment living close to the CBD.

Alice Springs offers a different proposition entirely, with a property market influenced by tourism, health services, and government employment. Prices in Alice Springs remain accessible, and rental demand is solid due to the limited supply of quality housing.

First home buyers in the NT benefit from stamp duty concessions and the Territory Home Owner Discount, which provides significant savings for eligible buyers purchasing established homes. The First Home Owner Grant is also available for new builds, making the NT one of the most incentive rich jurisdictions for new entrants.
