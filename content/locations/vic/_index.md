## Victorian first home buyer grants and concessions

Victoria offers several incentives, though the stamp duty rules are more complex than other states:

**First Home Owner Grant:** $10,000 for new homes valued up to $750,000. This applies to newly built homes, apartments, and townhouses. Established properties are not eligible.

**Stamp duty exemption:** First home buyers pay zero stamp duty on properties valued up to $600,000. A concession applies for properties between $600,000 and $750,000.

**Off the plan concession:** Purchasers of off the plan properties may receive a stamp duty concession based on the construction component, which can reduce the duty payable by thousands of dollars.

**First Home Guarantee:** Purchase with as little as 5% deposit without paying Lenders Mortgage Insurance. On a $700,000 Melbourne property, this could save you $15,000 to $25,000.

**Example:** A first home buyer purchasing a new $650,000 townhouse in Melbourne's western suburbs could receive the $10,000 FHOG, pay reduced stamp duty under the concession, and avoid LMI through the First Home Guarantee. Combined savings of roughly $30,000 to $40,000.

**Important: Victorian land tax for investors.** Victoria applies land tax to investment properties on a different basis to most other states. As of recent changes, all investment properties are subject to an additional surcharge. This affects your rental yield calculations and should be factored into any investment decision. We can help you understand the impact on your cash flow when structuring an investment loan.

## Victorian property market

Melbourne is Australia's second largest city, and its property market offers opportunities across a wide price spectrum.

**Inner and middle Melbourne:** Suburbs like Fitzroy, Carlton, Richmond, and Brunswick command premium prices but offer strong capital growth history. Apartments in these areas require careful lender selection, as some buildings have restrictions or limitations on the maximum number of investor loans a lender will approve.

**Growth corridors:** Melbourne's western suburbs (Werribee, Tarneit, Melton) and south east (Pakenham, Clyde, Officer) continue to expand with new estates. Many of these new builds qualify for the FHOG. Prices in these areas typically range from $500,000 to $750,000 for houses, making them accessible for first home buyers.

**Regional Victoria:** Geelong (Victoria's second largest city), Ballarat, Bendigo, and the Surf Coast have experienced significant price growth driven by remote work trends and improved transport links. The Geelong Fast Rail project is expected to further boost demand. Lender appetite for regional postcodes varies, and some apply additional restrictions on LVR.

**Melbourne apartments:** This is an area where lender selection matters most. Many Melbourne CBD and inner city apartment buildings have restrictions from certain lenders due to oversupply concerns, building defect histories (particularly post 2010 builds), or high investor concentrations. We know which lenders are comfortable with which buildings and can save you the frustration of declined applications.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do I need a local Victorian broker?**
No. Mortgage brokers are nationally licensed and access the same lenders regardless of location. Our digital process means you receive the same service whether you are in Melbourne or Brisbane.

**How much stamp duty will I pay in Victoria?**
For a non first home buyer purchasing a $800,000 property, stamp duty in Victoria is approximately $43,000. First home buyers pay zero stamp duty on properties up to $600,000 and reduced rates up to $750,000.

**What deposit do I need for a Melbourne property?**
Minimum 5% for most lenders. For a $700,000 property, that is $35,000. With less than 20% ($140,000), you will typically pay LMI unless eligible for a government guarantee. LMI on a $700,000 purchase with 10% deposit is approximately $12,000 to $18,000.

**Are Melbourne apartments harder to finance?**
Some are. Lenders assess apartment buildings individually, and factors like the number of units, building age, defect history, and investor concentration all affect approval. We pre assess your property against our lender panel before lodging an application, which avoids unnecessary declines and credit enquiries.

**Can you help with a Victorian construction loan?**
Yes. We work with lenders who offer competitive construction loan products and guide you through the progressive drawdown process. Construction loans require more documentation than standard purchases, and we help you prepare everything upfront to avoid delays.
