## VIC property market

Melbourne is Australia's second largest city and its property market reflects that scale and diversity. From the inner city terrace houses of Fitzroy and Carlton to the growing family suburbs of Werribee and Pakenham, there are opportunities at every price point.

Victoria experienced a period of price adjustment in recent years, which has created openings for buyers who were previously priced out of the market. Melbourne's strong population growth, driven by both interstate and international migration, continues to underpin long term demand for housing.

Regional Victoria has emerged as a popular alternative for those seeking lifestyle and value. Towns like Geelong, Ballarat, and Bendigo offer lower entry prices and have benefited from improved infrastructure and the rise of flexible work arrangements.

First home buyers in Victoria can access stamp duty exemptions for properties valued under certain thresholds, and concessions are available at higher price points. The Victorian government has also offered first home owner grants for new builds, making it easier to enter the market.

Investors in Victoria should be aware of land tax thresholds and rental regulations that can affect returns. We help you factor these considerations into your loan strategy so you make informed decisions.
