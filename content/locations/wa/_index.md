## WA property market

Perth has experienced a significant resurgence in recent years after a prolonged period of flat or declining prices following the end of the mining investment boom. Affordability compared to Sydney and Melbourne has drawn attention from interstate buyers and investors, and local demand has strengthened as population growth accelerates.

Popular areas for buyers include Joondalup, Rockingham, Mandurah, and the rapidly developing northern corridor. Inner city suburbs such as Subiaco, Leederville, and Victoria Park continue to attract professionals and young families.

Regional WA presents a mixed picture. Towns tied to the resources sector can see rapid price movements in both directions, while lifestyle regions like Margaret River and the South West coast offer steadier conditions. Understanding these patterns is important when selecting a loan product, particularly around loan to value ratios and lender risk appetites.

First home buyers in WA benefit from stamp duty exemptions on properties under certain values and the First Home Owner Grant for new builds. These incentives, paired with Perth's relative affordability, make Western Australia an attractive market for people entering homeownership.
