## NSW first home buyer grants and concessions

New South Wales offers several incentives for first home buyers:

**First Home Buyer Assistance Scheme:** No stamp duty on properties up to $800,000. A sliding concession applies for properties valued between $800,000 and $1,000,000. For a $900,000 home, the saving compared to the full stamp duty rate is approximately $17,000.

**First Home Owner Grant:** $10,000 for new homes valued up to $600,000. This applies to newly built houses, townhouses, and apartments. It does not apply to established properties.

**First Home Guarantee:** Purchase with as little as 5% deposit without paying Lenders Mortgage Insurance. On a $800,000 Sydney apartment, avoiding LMI could save you $20,000 to $35,000.

**Example:** A first home buyer purchasing a new $590,000 apartment in Western Sydney could receive the $10,000 FHOG, pay zero stamp duty under the assistance scheme, and avoid LMI through the First Home Guarantee. Combined savings of potentially $40,000+.

## NSW property market

New South Wales has the highest median property prices in Australia. Sydney's median house price sits above $1.4 million, which means getting your loan structure right has an outsized impact on your long term financial position.

**Sydney:** The inner west, lower north shore, and eastern suburbs remain the most expensive. Western Sydney suburbs like Penrith, Liverpool, and Campbelltown offer more affordable entry points with improving infrastructure including the new Metro West line. Apartments in high density areas require careful lender selection, as some lenders apply restrictions on building size, postcode, or unit size.

**Central Coast and Hunter:** Growing numbers of Sydney workers have relocated to the Central Coast and Hunter Valley, driven by remote work flexibility and significantly lower property prices. Towns like Gosford, Wyong, and Maitland offer houses at a fraction of Sydney prices while maintaining reasonable commuting distance.

**Regional NSW:** Areas like Wollongong, Orange, Bathurst, and the Byron Bay hinterland have seen strong price growth. Lender appetite for regional postcodes varies, and some apply additional LVR restrictions. We know which lenders are comfortable in each area.

## Why use a broker for an NSW purchase

The rate difference between the best and worst lenders on the market can exceed 1.5%. On a $900,000 loan (common in Sydney), that gap is roughly $800 per month in repayments, or nearly $10,000 per year.

Beyond rates, lender policies vary significantly for NSW buyers:

**Apartment lending:** If you are buying a unit in a building with more than 50 apartments, or in a building with a high investor concentration, some lenders will restrict your maximum LVR or decline the loan entirely. We identify which lenders are comfortable with your specific building before you apply.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do I need a broker in Sydney to buy in Sydney?**
No. Mortgage brokers are licensed nationally, and the lenders we access are the same ones available to any broker in Australia. Our digital process means you get the same service whether you are in Surry Hills or Sunnybank.

**How much deposit do I need for a Sydney property?**
Minimum 5% for most lenders. On a $900,000 property, that is $45,000. With less than 20% ($180,000), you will typically pay LMI unless you qualify for the First Home Guarantee. LMI on a Sydney purchase with 10% deposit can easily be $20,000 to $30,000, so exploring guarantee schemes is worthwhile.

**What is stamp duty on a $1 million property in NSW?**
For a non first home buyer, stamp duty on a $1,000,000 property in NSW is approximately $40,000. First home buyers pay reduced or zero stamp duty depending on the property value (nil up to $800,000, concession up to $1,000,000).

**Can you help with a NSW investment property if I live in Queensland?**
Yes, and it is one of our most common scenarios. Queensland based investors frequently buy in NSW for capital growth potential, and we handle everything remotely.
