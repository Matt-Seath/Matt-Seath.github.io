## TAS property market

Hobart experienced remarkable price growth over several years, transforming from one of Australia's most affordable capitals to a mid range market. While the pace of growth has moderated, Hobart remains attractive to buyers seeking a high quality of life, strong community feel, and relative value compared to Sydney and Melbourne.

Popular suburbs for buyers include Sandy Bay, Battery Point, North Hobart, and the growing northern suburbs around Glenorchy and Brighton. Launceston also offers excellent value and has attracted attention from investors looking for solid rental yields.

Tasmania's appeal extends beyond the cities. Lifestyle buyers are drawn to the East Coast, the Huon Valley, and the Tamar Valley, where scenic beauty meets a quieter pace of life. Regional properties can present unique lending challenges, and having a broker who understands lender policies for these areas is a genuine advantage.

First home buyers in Tasmania can access stamp duty concessions and the First Home Owner Grant for new builds. With lower median prices than most mainland capitals, Tasmania remains one of the more accessible states for entering the property market.
