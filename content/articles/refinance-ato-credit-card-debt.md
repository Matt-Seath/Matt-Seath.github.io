In many cases, yes.

If you have built equity in your property, it may be possible to refinance your home loan and release equity to pay out ATO tax debt.

This strategy is commonly used by:

- Self-employed business owners
- Contractors with tax liabilities
- Investors managing multiple financial commitments
- Borrowers facing ATO payment pressure

Instead of managing tax penalties, interest charges, and ongoing payment plans, refinancing can allow borrowers to clear the debt and replace it with a structured loan repayment.

However, lender policies vary, and eligibility depends on your income, equity position, and overall financial profile.

## Consolidating Credit Card Debt Into Your Mortgage

Credit cards often carry very high interest rates, typically between 18% and 22% per year.

When balances grow across multiple cards, repayments can quickly become difficult to manage.

Refinancing may allow you to combine credit card balances into your home loan, potentially offering:

- Lower interest rates
- A single monthly repayment
- Improved cash flow
- A clear repayment structure

Many clients also choose to close unused credit cards after consolidation, helping prevent debt from building up again.

## Example Scenario

A client approached Auri Finance with the following situation:

- $45,000 in credit card debt

- An existing mortgage balance of $520,000

By refinancing their home loan and accessing equity, we were able to consolidate the ATO debt and credit card balances into one loan.

The result:

- One manageable repayment
- Significantly lower interest compared with credit cards
- Improved monthly cash flow
- A clear path to paying down the debt

Every situation is different, but refinancing can sometimes provide a reset point for borrowers dealing with multiple debts.

## Important Things to Consider

Debt consolidation through refinancing can be effective, but it is important to understand the implications.

When unsecured debts such as credit cards or tax debt are added to a mortgage, they become secured against your property.

Borrowers should carefully consider:

- Whether the new loan structure is sustainable
- The long-term repayment strategy
- Avoiding future debt accumulation

A well structured refinance should focus on long term financial stability, not just short term relief.
