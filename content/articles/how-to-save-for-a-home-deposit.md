For most Australians, saving a home deposit is the single biggest barrier to getting into the property market. With property prices where they are, even a 10% deposit can feel like a mountain to climb. But with the right strategy, it is more achievable than you might think.

## How Much Do You Actually Need?

The traditional advice is to save 20% of the purchase price to avoid paying Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI). On a $600,000 property, that is $120,000, plus you will need extra for stamp duty, legal fees, and other costs.

However, many buyers purchase with far less:

- **5% deposit**: Possible through the Home Guarantee Scheme (no LMI required)
- **10% deposit**: A common starting point, though LMI will apply
- **20% deposit**: Avoids LMI entirely and often unlocks better interest rates

### Deposit Targets by Property Price

| Property Price | 5% Deposit | 10% Deposit | 20% Deposit |
|---|---|---|---|
| $500,000 | $25,000 | $50,000 | $100,000 |
| $600,000 | $30,000 | $60,000 | $120,000 |
| $700,000 | $35,000 | $70,000 | $140,000 |
| $800,000 | $40,000 | $80,000 | $160,000 |

Remember, you will also need approximately 3% to 5% of the purchase price on top of your deposit for stamp duty (unless exempt), conveyancing, inspections, and other upfront costs. For a $600,000 property, budget an additional $18,000 to $30,000 depending on your state and eligibility for concessions.

## 7 Strategies to Build Your Deposit Faster

### 1. Set a Clear Target and Timeline

Work backwards from the property price you are aiming for. Knowing exactly how much you need and by when makes it easier to create a realistic savings plan.

**Example:** If you need $60,000 (10% of $600,000) and want to buy in three years, you need to save roughly $1,670 per month, or about $385 per week.

### 2. Open a Dedicated Savings Account

Keep your deposit separate from your everyday spending in a high interest savings account. Many Australian banks offer bonus saver accounts with competitive rates when you meet monthly deposit conditions. Out of sight, out of mind, and earning interest.

### 3. Automate Your Savings

Set up an automatic transfer on payday so the money moves before you have a chance to spend it. Even small, consistent amounts add up over time.

| Weekly Savings | Monthly Total | Annual Total | 3 Year Total (at 5% interest) |
|---|---|---|---|
| $200 | $867 | $10,400 | ~$33,600 |
| $350 | $1,517 | $18,200 | ~$58,800 |
| $500 | $2,167 | $26,000 | ~$84,000 |
| $700 | $3,033 | $36,400 | ~$117,600 |

### 4. Reduce Your Biggest Expenses

Housing and transport are typically the two largest expenses. Consider whether downsizing your rental, getting a housemate, or reducing car costs could free up extra savings.

### 5. Use the First Home Super Saver Scheme (FHSS)

The FHSS is one of the most powerful tools available to first home buyers. It allows you to make voluntary contributions to your superannuation and withdraw them later for your home deposit.

**Key details of the FHSS:**

- **Voluntary contributions**: You can make concessional (before tax, salary sacrifice) or non concessional (after tax) contributions up to annual and total limits set by the government
- **Tax advantage**: Concessional contributions are taxed at 15% inside super rather than your marginal tax rate, which could be significantly higher
- **Withdrawal**: Concessional contributions are released with deemed earnings based on a rate set by the ATO

The contribution limits and deemed earnings rate are updated periodically, so check the [ATO website](https://www.ato.gov.au) for the latest figures.

**How the tax benefit works:**

If you are on a marginal tax rate above 15%, every dollar you salary sacrifice into super under the FHSS is taxed at 15% instead of your higher rate. Over several years of maximum contributions, the tax savings alone can add up to thousands of dollars depending on your income.

**Important:** To use the FHSS, you must apply to the ATO for a determination and then request a release of funds before signing a contract to purchase. Make sure you understand the process and timing requirements.

### 6. Look Into Government Grants

Depending on your state and whether you are buying new or established, you may be eligible for:

- **First Home Owner Grant (FHOG)**: Available in most states for eligible new home purchases, with grant amounts and property value caps varying by state. Check your state's revenue office for current details
- **Stamp duty concessions**: Reduced or waived stamp duty for first home buyers in most states, with eligibility thresholds varying by location
- **Home Guarantee Scheme**: Buy with as little as 5% deposit with no LMI

### 7. Pick Up Extra Income

A side hustle, overtime, or selling unused items can accelerate your savings. Directing any windfalls (tax returns, bonuses) straight into your deposit fund helps too.

## Do Not Forget the Extra Costs

Your deposit is not the only upfront cost. Budget for:

- **Stamp duty**: Varies by state and property value (unless you qualify for an exemption)
- **Conveyancing fees**: $1,000 to $3,000
- **Building and pest inspection**: $400 to $800
- **Loan application fees**: Varies by lender (often waived)
- **Moving costs**: Removalists, connections, and similar expenses

A good rule of thumb is to budget an extra 3% to 5% of the purchase price for these costs on top of your deposit.

## Putting It All Together: A Savings Roadmap

Here is an example for someone earning $85,000 who wants to buy a $600,000 home with a 10% deposit in three years:

| Source | Amount |
|---|---|
| Regular savings ($400/week for 3 years) | ~$62,400+ (plus interest) |
| FHSS contributions (max per year for 3 years, after tax benefit) | Varies by contribution limits |
| First Home Owner Grant (if eligible) | Varies by state |
| **Total available** | **Potentially well above your deposit target** |
| Less deposit needed (10%) | $60,000 |
| Less stamp duty and costs (~4%) | ~$24,000 |

The exact figures depend on current FHSS limits, your state's FHOG amount, and applicable stamp duty concessions. The key takeaway is that combining regular savings, the FHSS, and government grants can dramatically shorten your savings timeline. Your broker can help you model the numbers for your specific situation.
