Buying a home on a single income might seem daunting, but thousands of Australians do it every year. With the right planning and the right loan, solo buyers can absolutely get into the property market and build wealth on their own terms.

## How Does Borrowing Power Work for Single Buyers?

Lenders assess your borrowing capacity based on your income, expenses, debts, and deposit. As a single applicant, you have one income stream, which typically means a lower borrowing capacity compared to a couple, but that does not mean you cannot buy.

### Approximate Borrowing Capacity by Income

The table below gives a general indication of how much a single buyer might be able to borrow, assuming minimal existing debts, reasonable living expenses, and a standard 30 year variable loan. Actual figures vary by lender and individual circumstances.

| Gross Annual Income | Approximate Borrowing Capacity |
|---|---|
| $60,000 | $320,000 to $380,000 |
| $70,000 | $390,000 to $450,000 |
| $80,000 | $450,000 to $520,000 |
| $90,000 | $520,000 to $590,000 |
| $100,000 | $590,000 to $660,000 |
| $120,000 | $700,000 to $790,000 |

### Worked Example: Single Buyer on $90,000

Let us say you earn $90,000 per year (before tax) and have no existing debts. Here is how the numbers might work:

- **Gross income**: $90,000 per year
- **Estimated borrowing capacity**: Approximately $550,000
- **Deposit saved**: $60,000 (just under 11% of purchase price)
- **Target purchase price**: $550,000
- **Stamp duty (first home buyer in QLD)**: Approximately $10,000 (with concession)
- **Other purchase costs**: Approximately $3,000 (conveyancing, inspections, searches)
- **Total funds needed**: Approximately $73,000
- **Shortfall or surplus**: With $60,000 saved, you would need a small top up, or you could use the Home Guarantee Scheme with a 5% deposit ($27,500) and put the remaining funds towards stamp duty and costs

This example shows that a single buyer on $90,000 can realistically purchase an apartment or townhouse in many Australian suburbs.

Factors that help maximise your borrowing power:

- **Low existing debts**: Paying off personal loans, car loans, and credit cards before applying
- **Clean credit history**: Consistent repayment history shows lenders you are reliable
- **Stable income**: Permanent full time employment is viewed most favourably, though other income types are considered
- **Minimal living expenses**: Lenders assess your declared expenses against benchmarks like the Household Expenditure Measure (HEM)
- **No buy now pay later debts**: Services like Afterpay and Zip reduce your borrowing power even if the balances are small

## Strategies for Single Buyers

### 1. Start With What You Can Afford

You do not need to buy your dream home first. Many single buyers start with an apartment or townhouse and upgrade later as their equity and income grow. A well located unit in a growth suburb can be an excellent stepping stone.

### 2. Take Advantage of Government Schemes

Single buyers are fully eligible for all first home buyer incentives:

- **First Home Owner Grant**: Available in most states for eligible new home purchases (in QLD, this is $30,000 for new homes valued under $750,000)
- **Home Guarantee Scheme**: Buy with as little as 5% deposit without paying LMI. Places are allocated specifically for single buyers, with property price caps varying by location
- **Stamp duty concessions**: Reduced or waived in many states for first home buyers
- **First Home Super Saver Scheme**: Salary sacrifice into super and withdraw for your deposit with tax benefits, allowing you to save faster using pre tax income

### 3. Consider a Guarantor

A family member can act as a guarantor by offering their property as additional security. This can help you avoid LMI and borrow more than your deposit alone would allow. The guarantor does not give you money. They provide security that reduces the lender's risk. Once you have built enough equity (typically 80% loan to value ratio), the guarantee can be released.

### 4. Look at Different Locations

If your budget is tight in your preferred suburb, consider nearby areas that offer better value. Proximity to transport, schools, and amenities can mean strong growth potential even in more affordable suburbs. Looking just 10 to 15 minutes further from the city centre can open up significantly more options.

### 5. Get Preapproval Early

Preapproval gives you a clear picture of your budget and shows vendors you are a serious buyer. This is especially important as a solo buyer. You want to move confidently when you find the right property.

## Common Concerns for Solo Buyers

**"What if interest rates go up?"**
Budget for rate rises by stress testing your repayments. If you can comfortably afford repayments at 2% above your current rate, you will be well positioned. Lenders already assess your capacity at a buffer of around 3% above the loan rate.

**"What if I lose my job?"**
Building an emergency fund (3 to 6 months of expenses) before buying provides a safety net. Income protection insurance is also worth considering.

**"Should I wait until I am earning more?"**
Waiting means property prices may also rise. Getting into the market sooner, even with a smaller property, lets you start building equity rather than paying someone else's mortgage through rent.
