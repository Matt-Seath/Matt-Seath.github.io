You have found the perfect property, your loan has been approved, and the contracts are signed. Now comes settlement day, the final step where ownership officially transfers to you. But what actually happens on the day, and what do you need to do?

## What Is Settlement?

Settlement is the legal process where the property's title is transferred from the seller to the buyer. Your lender releases the funds to the seller's bank, and in return, you receive the keys to your new home.

In most states, settlement takes place 30 to 90 days after contracts are exchanged, depending on what was agreed in the contract of sale. In Queensland, the standard settlement period is typically 30 to 42 days.

## What Happens Behind the Scenes

Settlement involves several parties working together:

- **Your solicitor or conveyancer** prepares and reviews all legal documents
- **Your lender** arranges the transfer of funds
- **The seller's solicitor** confirms that all conditions have been met
- **Your state's land titles office** records the change of ownership

### Electronic Settlement via PEXA

In most Australian states, settlement is now handled electronically through PEXA (Property Exchange Australia). PEXA is a secure digital platform that allows all parties, including lenders, conveyancers, and government bodies, to complete the settlement process online in real time. This means your conveyancer, the seller's conveyancer, and both lenders can all connect to the same digital workspace and exchange documents and funds simultaneously.

Electronic settlement through PEXA has made the process faster and more reliable. Funds are transferred, titles are registered, and stamp duty is lodged all within the same transaction window, typically completing in under an hour on the scheduled day. There is no need for anyone to physically meet at a settlement room, which was the old process.

## Your Settlement Day Checklist

### Before Settlement

- **Final inspection**: Conduct a final walk through of the property (usually the day before or morning of settlement) to make sure it is in the agreed condition. Check that all included fixtures and fittings are present and that no damage has occurred since you last inspected
- **Confirm with your conveyancer**: Ensure all documents are signed and funds are in order
- **Arrange insurance**: Your building insurance should be active from the date of settlement. If you are buying an apartment, confirm that the body corporate insurance is in place and arrange contents insurance separately
- **Organise utility connections**: Arrange for electricity, gas, water, and internet to be connected in your name from the settlement date

### On Settlement Day

- **Stay available**: Your conveyancer or broker may need to reach you if any last minute issues arise
- **Wait for confirmation**: You will receive a call or message from your conveyancer once settlement has been completed
- **Collect the keys**: Usually from the real estate agent's office once settlement is confirmed

## Common Settlement Delays

Settlement does not always go smoothly. Some common issues include:

- **Late bank paperwork**: If the lender's documents are not ready on time
- **Outstanding rates or levies**: Unpaid council rates or body corporate fees by the seller
- **Condition issues**: Problems found during the final inspection that need resolving
- **Title issues**: Discrepancies in the title or unregistered caveats
- **Funds not cleared**: If your deposit or other funds have not cleared in time

If settlement is delayed, your conveyancer will negotiate with the other party to resolve the issue as quickly as possible. In most contracts, there are penalty provisions for late settlement, which can work in your favour if the delay is on the seller's side.

## What Happens After Settlement

Once settlement is confirmed:

1. **You own the property**: Congratulations!
2. **Your loan repayments begin**: Typically on the first scheduled date after settlement
3. **Keys are released**: Collect them from the agent
4. **Title is registered**: The land titles office updates ownership records (this happens automatically through PEXA)
5. **Change your address**: Update your details with banks, insurers, employers, and government agencies
