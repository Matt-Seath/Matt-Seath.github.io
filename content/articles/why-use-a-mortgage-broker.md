When it is time to get a home loan, many people head straight to their bank. It makes sense, as you already have an account there and they know your finances. But there is a reason more than 70% of all Australian home loans are now written through mortgage brokers. According to the Mortgage and Finance Association of Australia (MFAA), broker market share has grown consistently over the past decade and now accounts for approximately seven in every ten home loans settled in Australia.

## What Does a Mortgage Broker Actually Do?

A mortgage broker acts as an intermediary between you and lenders. Instead of you approaching banks one by one, your broker compares loans across a wide panel of lenders, including major banks, smaller banks, credit unions, and nonbank lenders, to find the best option for your situation.

They handle the research, paperwork, and negotiations so you do not have to.

## Bank vs Broker: A Direct Comparison

| Feature | Going Direct to a Bank | Using a Mortgage Broker |
|---|---|---|
| Number of products available | Only that bank's own products | 30 to 50+ lenders, hundreds of products |
| Cost to you | Free | Free (broker is paid by the lender) |
| Rate negotiation | Limited, you get what is offered | Broker negotiates across lenders on your behalf |
| Time investment | High, you must research and apply to each bank | Low, broker does the comparison and paperwork |
| Ongoing support | Branch staff may change | Same broker relationship from start to settlement |
| Knowledge of policies | Knows only their own policies | Knows policies across dozens of lenders |
| Advice obligation | Must recommend their own products | Must act in your best interest (Best Interest Duty) |
| Complex situations | May decline and send you elsewhere | Can match you with a lender suited to your situation |
| After hours availability | Limited to branch hours | Many brokers offer evening and weekend appointments |

## The Advantages of Using a Broker

### Access to More Options

Your bank can only offer you their own products. A broker typically has access to 30 to 50+ lenders and hundreds of loan products. This means you are far more likely to find a competitive rate and the right loan features for your needs.

### It Is Free for You

In most cases, mortgage brokers are paid a commission by the lender, not by you. You get professional advice and loan comparison at no cost. The lender pays the broker a commission (typically around 0.6% of the loan amount upfront plus a smaller trailing commission) for bringing them the business.

### Best Interest Duty

Since January 2021, mortgage brokers in Australia have been legally required to act in your best interest under the Best Interest Duty. This means your broker must recommend a loan that is suitable for you, not one that pays them the highest commission. This legal obligation does not apply to bank staff, who are only required to recommend products that are "not unsuitable."

### They Save You Time

Instead of filling out multiple applications and comparing products yourself, your broker does the legwork. They know which lenders suit different borrower profiles and can steer you away from lenders likely to decline your application, saving you from unnecessary credit enquiries on your file.

### Expert Guidance Through the Process

From pre-approval to settlement, your broker guides you through every step. They explain the jargon, help you understand the fine print, and advocate on your behalf if issues arise.

### They Know the Policies

Every lender has different policies around income types, property types, LVR thresholds, and credit history. A good broker knows these inside out and matches you with the lender most likely to approve your loan on favourable terms. This is especially valuable if your situation does not fit the standard mould.

## When a Broker Is Especially Valuable

- **First home buyers** navigating the process for the first time and unfamiliar with the jargon, grants, and steps involved
- **Self employed borrowers** whose income is assessed differently and may require specific lenders who understand business financials
- **Property investors** who need strategic loan structuring to optimise tax benefits and cash flow
- **Refinancers** wanting to compare their current deal against the market and ensure switching actually saves money after all costs
- **Borrowers with complex situations** such as multiple income sources, existing debts, or a less than perfect credit history

## The Numbers Behind Broker Growth

The growth in broker usage tells a clear story about the value they provide:

- Over **70%** of all home loans in Australia are now written through brokers (MFAA data)
- Broker market share has grown from around 55% a decade ago to over 70% today
- Customer satisfaction ratings for brokers consistently outperform bank channels
- Brokers settle tens of billions of dollars in home loans each quarter across Australia

This growth reflects the fact that borrowers who use brokers generally access more competitive rates and a wider range of products than those who go direct to a single lender.

## Questions to Ask Your Broker

1. How many lenders do you have on your panel?
2. How are you paid, and does it vary by lender?
3. What information do you need from me to get started?
4. How long will the process take from application to settlement?
5. Will you help me through to settlement and beyond?
6. Do you offer ongoing loan reviews to make sure I stay on a competitive rate?
